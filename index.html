About Me

Hi there! My name is Evangelos. Deeply interested in Human Computer Interaction, I would introduce my self as "A computer scientist with an artistic point of view". Informatics assembles the way I am biologically thinking, and building bottom-up solutions after analyzing goals to smaller tasks have always been my attribute. However I am passionate for Dance, an activity that started since my childhood and turned out to become a major part of me. I am dancing Balkan folklore dances, Contemporary and Ballet. The conciliatory force between those two aforementioned parts, Informatics and Dance, is Music. And I am involved into it both as Lute player bus also as a Sound engineer. It brings together those two different worlds and combines them in a unique and dynamic way.

I studied Informatics & Telecommunications at the University of Athens and my BSc thesis was published at HCI International 2013. In 2012 I enrolled to the University of Copenhagen, Denmark for a MSc in Computer Science. Living in such a multi-international environment enhanced my personality and trained me to work and collaborate under demanding conditions. My MSc Thesis, introduces Fat Finger in an attempt to utilize the contact size of the finger touching the screen as an additional input dimension, in mobile devices. My working experience both in Denmark and at CERN in Switzerland taught me how to work efficiently and how to become very detail oriented. The experience gained in such a multi-international organization was massive and positively expanded my horizons. I am a sociable person with strong communicational skills and I can be easily adjusted in new environments, something that is amplified by my art-related activities, with which I have traveled around the world, established international friendships and been exposed to numerous demanding conditions where my performance was of major importance.

Acoustic Recording's Home Studio


The Situation

Acoustic Recording's Home Studio is located in Egaleo, Athens and it consists of 2 separate -Acoustically Treated- rooms (9 sq.m. and 4 sq.m. respectively). The larger one acts both as a recording but also as a mixing room, where all the equipment is settled, while the smaller one is especially designed for Instrument and Vocal Recordings. The wooden floor, bass traps, foam absorbers, diffusers along with the extra wooden parts placed on the walls, all constitute and even reinforce a very natural sound atmosphere inside both rooms.


Applications

The studio can be used either for individuals recordings, thus each musician is recorded separately, or it can host up to 3-4 musician performing and being recorded concurrently. If electric instruments are to be used, then depending on the setup an extra space can be used hosting up to 6-7 people in total.
